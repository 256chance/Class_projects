Pros & Cons

  • Arbitrage Pros

    • -Low Risk, Once the bets are placed on both sides you're guranteed a profit unless a sportsbook voids a bet which is not likely to happen

    -Avoid Variance, by Arb betting you avoide the bankroll rollercoasters that EV betting has because the outcome of each side doesn't matter

  • Arbitrage Cons

    • -You have to be quick. Odds move fast, like the stock market they adjust to news and other things. They are only getting faster with technology getting better each day

    -Sports books will limit you faster. Sportsbooks aren't dumb, they know when they are being beat and will do what's neccessary for them to make more money. Just like a casino.

  • Expected Value Pros

    • -Higher Profitability, You can expect to make more money in the long run EV betting because you're betting with an edge.

    -No emotional decesion making, you're only following the numbers. You aren't betting off of a hunch. The numbers don't lie.

  • Expected Value Cons

    • -Variance, it's possible that all the bets you place that day lose. It happens. You need to be prepared for it because when you win big you can lose big just as easily.

    -It's a skill and it takes time to learn and it can be costly to mess up. It can take up alot of your time and you might not get the results you want right away.
